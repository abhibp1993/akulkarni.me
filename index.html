About me

I am a second year Ph.D. student in Robotics Engineering Program at Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI), advised by Prof. Jie Fu. My broad research interest is in Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Decision Making for autonomous agents. Specifically, I am interested in problems at the intersection of the following areas;

  • Formal Methods - Synthesis (a.k.a. Reactive Synthesis or Automatic Controller Synthesis)

  • Formal Logic: Temporal, Epistemic and Spatial Logic

  • Game and Hypergame Theory

My research focuses on developing algorithms that synthesize provably-correct strategies for autonomous agents to accomplish their tasks in dynamic and uncontrollable environments; which may contain a number of cooperative, non-cooperative or even adversarial agents. I explore applications of the above theory to

  • Mobile Robots: Mission and Motion Planning

  • Cybersecurity

  • Multi-robot and Human-robot Interactions

I received by Bachelors degree in Electronics and Telecommunications Engineering from Vishwakarma Institute of Technology (VIT), Pune, India. During my undergraduate studies, I was fortunate to work with Prof. Pushkar Joglekar and Prof. Satish Inamdar on robot motion planning and cyber-physical systems that laid the foundation for my present research. I also started a student organization - Cognitive Robotics and Intelligent SysTems Lab (CRISTL) along with my colleague Monica Patel. At CRISTL, we developed and fabricated an autonomous robot Curio to be used for teaching at VIT.
